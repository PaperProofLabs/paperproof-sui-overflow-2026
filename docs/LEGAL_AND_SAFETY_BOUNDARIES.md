# Legal and Safety Boundaries

This submission repository summarizes PaperProof for Sui Overflow 2026. It is
not the sole implementation repository and does not relicense linked
repositories.

PaperProof Protocol refers to the open protocol layer and official deployed
protocol instances. PaperProof Labs refers to the originating team and
maintainer of the official interface, SDKs, reference indexer, documentation,
and brand identity.

## User Content and Protocol Use

PaperProof Protocol is infrastructure for durable, verifiable, and
community-governed artifacts. Users and third-party interfaces are responsible
for the artifacts, files, metadata, comments, votes, proposals, links, uploads,
and transactions they create or submit.

Display of protocol records in an app, demo, indexer, Explorer view, or
submission material does not by itself mean that PaperProof Labs created,
reviewed, approved, endorsed, or recommends that record.

## Interface and Takedown Boundary

Official PaperProof interfaces may hide, delist, label, de-rank, refuse to
preview, or stop caching content or links for abuse, safety, copyright,
trademark, privacy, fraud, malware, spam, or policy reasons.

Because the project uses Sui and Walrus, PaperProof Labs may not be able to
delete underlying on-chain records, third-party records, or decentralized
storage content. Interface-level non-display is not the same as global
deletion.

## No Advice or Warranty

PaperProof materials, apps, SDKs, indexers, Copilot responses, and demos do
not provide legal, financial, investment, tax, medical, academic, peer-review,
security, or professional advice. Users should review wallet transactions and
make independent judgments.

Reports may be sent to paperproof.labs@gmail.com.
